Potato Leaf Disease Classifier

Android Studio | Java | Python | TensorFlow

The
Rationale

The Philippines faces particular challenges in both food and nutrition security. Despite being a significant agricultural producer, the country falls below the global average in The Economist's 2022 Global Food Security Index.

Potatoes plays an essential role in addressing food and nutrition challenges. They are not just starch-rich vegetables; they are packed with carbohydrates, fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making them crucial in combating malnutrition

The Philippines' potato industry faces significant challenges. Pest and disease outbreaks, poor soil quality, and a shortage of seed tubers pose obstacles to the sector's growth and resilience. Potato diseases, particularly late blight, have a history of causing widespread damage to crops and economies, with the disease continuing to impact yields and economic well-being in Southeast Asia and other regions.

This mobile application prototype was developed to support precision agriculture efforts and help solve these problems. The app utilizes multiple machine learning models to classify potato leaf diseases in offline environments which ensures accessibility in remote farmlands without internet connectivity.

The Application

The development of mobile application for the potato leaf disease classifier using multiple machine learning models helps precision agriculture. Unlike existing technologies, the mobile application is accessible via small devices like smartphones which simplifies interaction for users.
Furthermore, it operates without requiring an internet connection, making it particularly well-suited for countries like the Philippines, where reliable internet access may be limited in remote areas.

Application Guide

  • Classified Photo - Displays the image classified by the model using the application. The image can either be pre-existing from the gallery or captured using the device's camera.

  • Prediction Result - Shows the predicted class and the confidence level of the prediction. If the image is not a potato leaf, it displays "Undefined."

  • Capture Photo - Enables the device's camera to capture a photo for classification. The captured photo is saved to the gallery.

  • Select Image - Allows users to choose a pre-existing image from the device's gallery for classification.

The Classifications

The mobile application is capable of identify potato leaves and predicting three classes: healthy leaf, early blight leaf, and late blight leaf.

Healthy Leaf - Smooth and slightly waxy to touch. Prominent veins running along the underside of the leaflet, branching out from central midrib. Leaves should be free of any holes, yellowing, browning, or wilting
Early Blight - Numerous small, round, oval or angular, dark brown to black, dry, and papery necrotic spots which have angular margins appear on leaves.
Late Blight - Leaves have dark brown spots, water-soaked irregular pale green lesions. White mold-bearing spores develop around these spots during humid weather conditions.
